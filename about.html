About Me

Hey, I’m Meg! I’m a small-town girl from Farmington Hills, Michigan who packed her bags and headed to the big city of Los Angeles—just trying to make it in this wild, spinning world we call Earth.

I kicked off my career as a software developer, but quickly realized that coding for a corporation forever wasn’t it. I’ve been a dancer, musician, and artist for most of my life, and choosing a strictly technical path felt like I was betraying a huge part of myself. So here I am now—pursuing what Dr. Dre would call the “degree of disruption” at the Iovine and Young Academy at USC.

As I’ve grown both professionally and creatively, I’ve discovered that my sweet spot is in crafting and enhancing experiences. I’ve dipped my toes in a wide range of skills, and while that can be overwhelming, I’ve learned that success comes from taking the leap, picking a direction, and making it yours. I’m now channeling all those skills into my passion for the entertainment industry—where storytelling, emotion, and innovation collide.

Lately, I’ve been diving deep into visual effects and aspiring to become a true master in the space. I want to amplify the stories that matter—creating visuals that not only captivate the eyes but resonate with the soul.

Outside of work and school, I’m a big lover of music, dance, skateboarding, and animation. I play piano, violin, guitar, and bass (yes, I’m basically a one-woman band), and I live for spontaneous jam sessions and learning new choreo. And hey, if you’re down for the skatepark—I’ll join (just don’t expect pro tricks yet, haha). I’m also always down to chat about anime, cartoons, or the best soundtracks in animated history.

Let’s make something magical—or at least really, really fun.
